My Strategy for FMT (Click to Open)

A lot of you asked me how I studied FMT and managed to score 70% in 3rd-year MBBS, so here's exactly what I did. For the Forensic Medicine part, I mainly used Biswas. Honestly, it's a good book and I never felt the need to switch to anything else. My biggest advice would be: don't try to read everything. Start with your PYQs and identify the frequently asked topics. Read those topics properly from the book and focus on understanding them well.

At the same time, don't compromise your PG entrance preparation. You may or may not watch all the Marrow videos, but do go through the concepts. If Marrow explains a topic better or mentions an important point that's not in the book, simply annotate it in your book.

Now coming to Toxicology — my approach was completely different. Toxicology is vast, and although it doesn't contribute a major chunk of university exams, it's still important. I initially used Marrow notes, but I felt they weren't sufficient for university answer writing.

So I used the Marrow notes as my base and then added important points from the standard textbook, PYQs, and wherever I found concepts explained more clearly. Over time, these became heavily annotated notes with all the university-relevant content in one place.

A lot of people have been asking for these Toxicology notes, so I've decided to share them.
